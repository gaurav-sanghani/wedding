Examples of Indian outfits

Lehenga

A long skirt accompanied by a matching blouse and dupatta/odni (long scarf). Women usually wear these because the blouses are typically very elaborate and decorative. The dupatta is used to cover some of the torso, while still exposing the decorative detail of the blouse.

How to wear a lehenga
Salwar Kameez/Anarkali

Popular because they fit any body type and are very easy to wear. The anarkali (long tunic) is worn with loose or tight-fitting pants, known as churidars. A Salwaar Kameez can be worn without a dupatta, but more often than not, women wear a matching dupatta

Saree (Sari)

The most elegant, but also a more difficult outfit to wear. Length of fabric varies from seven to nine meters with different styles of draping the sari (all of the regions in India drape sari slightly differently). The sari blouse typically is less elaborate than that of the lehenga because most of it will usually be covered. However, sari blouses are known as being very versatile in cuts, necklines, and backlines.
